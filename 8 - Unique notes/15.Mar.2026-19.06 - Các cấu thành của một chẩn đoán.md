# Main contents
A **complete diagnosis** often contains:
### 1. Disease name
The main pathology.
Example:
- Acute appendicitis
- Community-acquired pneumonia
---
### 2. Etiology (if known)
Cause of the disease.
Examples:
- Bacterial
- Traumatic
- Viral
- Idiopathic
Example:
- Bacterial pneumonia due to _Streptococcus pneumoniae_
---
### 3. Location / organ involved
Where the disease occurs.
Examples:
- Right lower lobe pneumonia
- Left femoral fracture
- Right lower quadrant appendicitis
---
### 4. Severity / stage / classification
If the disease has staging or grading.
Examples:
- Mild / moderate / severe
- Stage II
- Grade III
- Complicated / uncomplicated

---
### 5. Course / timing
How the disease is evolving.
Examples:
- Acute
- Chronic
- Recurrent
- Subacute
---
### 6. Complications
Important complications caused by the disease.
Examples:
- With septic shock
- With perforation
- With peritonitis
---
### 7. Associated conditions / comorbidities
Other diseases that affect management.
Examples:
- With hypertension
- With diabetes mellitus
---

✅ **General formula**

**(Course) + (Etiology) + (Disease) + (Location) + (Severity/Stage) + (Complications) + (Comorbidities)**

---

### Example 1 (Surgery)

**Acute gangrenous appendicitis with localized peritonitis**

Fuller version:

> Acute gangrenous appendicitis with localized peritonitis, postoperative day 0 after appendectomy.

---

### Example 2 (Pediatrics)

> Severe community-acquired pneumonia of the right lower lobe with respiratory failure.

---

### Example 3 (Internal medicine)

> Acute inferior ST-elevation myocardial infarction complicated by cardiogenic shock in a patient with hypertension and type 2 diabetes.

---

### Example 4 (Trauma)

> Closed displaced fracture of the mid-shaft of the right femur due to traffic accident.

---

💡 **Clinical tip (important in exams):**

Good diagnoses usually answer **5 questions**:

1. **What disease?**
2. **Where?**
3. **Cause?**
4. **How severe?**
5. **Any complications?**
